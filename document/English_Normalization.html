Steps for Normalization

Goal: To correct for unwanted technical variation in protein expression data that may arise from experimental batch effects

  • Improving the accuracy of differential expression analysis
  • Reducing the impact of batch effects
  • Enhancing the reproducibility of their results

  1. Click [Batch Normalization] tab in the side bar on the left of this page.

  2. Select the normalization method:

  • If your data needs to normalize based on internal sample, choose internal.
  • If your data doesnt have internal sample, choose linear.
  1. Input your batch group information in the text area then click [Run Batch Normalization] to start normalization process. Please include header in your batch group.
  • If you choose internal method, please use the third column as the internal information to specify the internal sample.
  • If you choose linear method, you only need two columns to indicate the batch groups.

  1. After normalization, [Result Table] will show on the right of the page. [Intensity Distribution] will be drawn simultaneously.

  2. Next, you can choose to do Covariance Correction or directly start differential expression analysis.
